How to Help

Since our current goal is to pass a bill in the state legislature, there's literally only one thing anyone can do to help. And that is to help convince the legislators to pass the bill.

The best way to do that is to contact your two representatives and ask them to support the bill on your behalf. Remember, they work for you. Their job is to represent you. So enough of us tell them that we want them to support this bill, they will have no choice but to listen.

Which contact method is best?

Anything is better than nothing! But some communication channels are more effective than others. Phone and physical letters mailed through snail-mail are best. Emails and other digital forms of communication have a chance of getting lost in the noise.

What should I say?

You can keep your message short, especially if it's a phone call. We don't want to waste anyone's time, we just want to make our support known.

Here's an example of what you might say:

Hi this is (name) and I live at XYZ in your district. I wanted to ask you to support a bill from Representative Jonathan Singer that will introduce Approval Voting to Colorado. Approval Voting is a voting method that will help make our elections more fair. You can find out more at coloradoapproves.org or av4co.org. If you like the idea, Rep Singer also needs more co sponsors for the bill. Thanks.
